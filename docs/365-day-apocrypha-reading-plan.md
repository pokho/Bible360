# 365-Day Chronological Reading Plan for Pseudepigrapha, Apocrypha, and Deuterocanonical Texts

## Overview

This comprehensive 365-day reading plan provides a scholarly journey through the non-canonical Jewish and Christian texts from the Second Temple period through late antiquity. The plan is structured chronologically and thematically, offering readers a deep understanding of the development of Jewish thought, early Christian theology, and gnostic traditions.

## Structure

The reading plan is divided into four main phases:

### Phase 1: Deuterocanonical Foundation (Days 1-90)
**Period: 350 BCE - 100 BCE**
Focus on the books that form the foundation of the deuterocanonical tradition, wisdom literature, and historical narratives that shaped Second Temple Judaism.

#### Key Texts:
- **Tobit** (Days 1-7): Persian period narrative with themes of divine providence, family loyalty, and angelic mediation
- **Judith** (Days 8-15): Hellenistic period story of national deliverance and female heroism
- **Wisdom of Solomon** (Days 16-25): Hellenistic-Egyptian philosophical theology addressing immortality and divine wisdom
- **Sirach/Ecclesiasticus** (Days 26-43): Early Hellenistic period wisdom sayings and ethical instruction
- **Baruch** (Days 44-46): Post-exilic prophetic consolation and wisdom literature
- **1 & 2 Maccabees** (Days 47-63): Hasmonean period historical narratives of the Maccabean revolt

#### Scholarly Features:
- Mainstream academic dating (Charlesworth, OTP consensus)
- Transparent scholarly references
- Text type classification (40% wisdom, 30% historical, 20% apocalyptic, 10% liturgical)
- Cultural and historical context for each reading

### Phase 2: Old Testament Pseudepigrapha (Days 91-180)
**Period: 300 BCE - 100 CE**
Exploration of expanded biblical traditions, apocalyptic literature, and theological developments in Second Temple Judaism.

#### Key Texts:
- **1 Enoch** (Days 91-103): Early Second Temple apocalyptic visions, cosmology, and eschatology
- **Jubilees** (Days 104-115): Mid-Second Temple period "rewritten Bible" with festival calendar and purity laws
- **Testaments of the Twelve Patriarchs** (Days 116-122): Late Second Testament ethical testaments with messianic expectations
- **Psalms of Solomon** (Days 123-128): Roman period psalms responding to Pompey's invasion of Jerusalem
- **2 Baruch** (Days 129-134): Post-70 CE apocalyptic response to the destruction of Jerusalem
- **4 Ezra/2 Esdras** (Days 135-150): Post-70 CE theological response to national catastrophe

#### Scholarly Features:
- Emphasis on Jewish apocalyptic and messianic traditions
- Cross-textual thematic connections
- Development of afterlife and resurrection concepts
- Influence on early Christian thought

### Phase 3: Early Christianity & Apostolic Fathers (Days 151-270)
**Period: 50-200 CE**
Development of early Christian theology, church organization, and the formulation of orthodox doctrine.

#### Key Texts:
- **Didache** (Days 151-154): Early Christian manual on ethics, ritual, and community life
- **1 Clement** (Days 155-165): Late 1st century letter on church unity and apostolic authority
- **Letters of Ignatius** (Days 161-167): Early 2nd century letters on church unity and martyrdom
- **Polycarp's Letter to the Philippians** (Days 168-169): Early 2nd century Christian instruction
- **2 Clement** (Days 171-173): Mid-2nd century Christian sermon on repentance
- **Martyrdom of Polycarp** (Days 174-176): Account of early Christian martyrdom theology

#### Scholarly Features:
- Development of church hierarchy and structure
- Formation of Christian orthodoxy
- Theological development on Christology and soteriology
- Relationship between Judaism and emerging Christianity

### Phase 4: New Testament Apocrypha & Gnostic Texts (Days 241-365)
**Period: 70-400 CE**
Exploration of diverse Christian traditions, gnostic cosmologies, and alternative Christian narratives.

#### Key Texts:
- **Shepherd of Hermas** (Days 241-244): Mid-2nd century apocalyptic visions and ethical instruction
- **Epistle of Barnabas** (Days 245-246): Early 2nd century allegorical interpretation of Hebrew scriptures
- **Gospel of Thomas** (Days 247-251): Mid-2nd century sayings gospel with Gnostic elements
- **Protoevangelium of James** (Days 252-254): Mid-2nd century infancy gospel
- **Gospel of Peter** (Days 255-256): Late 2nd century passion and resurrection narrative
- **Acts of Paul and Thecla** (Days 257-258): Late 2nd century apocryphal acts
- **Nag Hammadi Library Selections** (Days 259-365): Comprehensive survey of Gnostic literature

#### Scholarly Features:
- Diversity of early Christian traditions
- Gnostic cosmologies and soteriologies
- Alternative portraits of Jesus and early Christian figures
- Development of Christian orthodoxy versus heresy

## Academic Standards

### Dating Methodology
- Mainstream scholarly consensus (Charlesworth OTP, Meyer, Fitzmyer, Nickelsburg)
- Conservative dating ranges reflecting scholarly debate
- Transparent uncertainty acknowledgment
- Regular updates as scholarship progresses

### Text Classification
- **Deuterocanonical**: Books included in Catholic/Orthodox canons
- **Pseudepigraphal**: Jewish texts outside biblical canon
- **Patristic**: Early Christian church father writings
- **NT Apocrypha**: Christian texts expanding New Testament narratives
- **Gnostic**: Texts from Gnostic tradition discovered at Nag Hammadi

### Source Traditions
- **Catholic/Orthodox**: Recognized deuterocanonical tradition
- **Academic**: Mainstream scholarly consensus
- **Manuscript Evidence**: Based on actual manuscript discoveries
